What is stereotype threat?

Stereotype threat is the risk of confirming negative stereotypes about their social group. This is one of the topics that have been widely studied in the field of psychology. One example to illustrate this is the experiment conducted by Steele and Aronson in 1995, where African Americans perform worse than their White peers on the SAT when their race is emphasized. Most importantly, the individual doesn't need to subscribe to the stereotype for it to be activated.

Fortunately, I have not experienced this in my life.

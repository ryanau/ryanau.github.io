For me, I think respect is something crucial in my life. Having respect to someone shows that you acknowledge the other person's presence and care about his/her dignity. Simple actions such as making eye contact, saying hi to them when you walk pass them will make them feel like they're valued by you. This is very important in maintaining positive relationships with people even though you might not necessarily be close to them. This creates a good impression.

Advice

The last time someone asked me for advice was probably on the topic of stress. People seem to see me as a person that manages stress well. Some of the things I do to destress is to exercise and smile! Have a beer or two helps too, but I would recommend drinking it while hanging out with your friends. This makes you realize and treasure the fact that there are people you can lean on and talk to.

Values and Stereotype Threat

I think that respecting people will provide you long-term benefit in terms of your interperson relationships.
